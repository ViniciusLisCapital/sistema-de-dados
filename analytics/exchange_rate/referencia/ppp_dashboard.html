What this model is

A Bayesian linear regression explaining the month-to-month change in the PPP deviation (tab 1) from four candidate channels — carry, terms-of-trade, long-term inflation expectations, and fiscal risk — each lagged one month. Deliberately the simpler "attempt one", ahead of a possible Kalman-filter state-space model ("attempt two") that would let these relationships vary over time instead of holding them fixed. Full design rationale and discussion: models/bayesian_deviation_model.md.

1 · Dependent variable

D(t) = 100 · ln( ptax(t) / equilibrium(t) )

The PPP deviation from tab 1 (any base month gives identical results here — only the intercept shifts, see the .md's derivation).

2 · Why first differences

ADF + KPSS on D(t), carry, tot → I(1)
breakeven, fiscal → I(0)

Regressing an I(1) series on a mix of I(1)/I(0) regressors risks spurious correlation. Pre-registered rule: if any series is I(1), difference everything uniformly.

3 · Regression equation

Δdev(t) = α + β₁Δcarry(t−1) + β₂Δtot(t−1)
+ β₃Δbreakeven(t−1) + β₄Δfiscal(t−1) + ε(t)

All four regressors standardized (mean 0, sd 1) before fitting, so β's are comparable in "per 1 SD of the regressor" units.

4 · Priors & likelihood

α ~ Normal(0,10)  β ~ Normal(0,2)
σ ~ HalfNormal(5)  ε ~ Normal(0,σ) or StudentT(ν,0,σ)

Weakly-informative priors on standardized regressors. Normal vs. Student-t errors compared via LOO (see below) — Normal preferred, no evidence of fat tails at this sample size.
