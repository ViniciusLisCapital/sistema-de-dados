# Monetary Policy — Bibliography Candidates

**Purpose:** first-pass candidate literature list for the monetary policy agent, built following `BIBLIOGRAPHY_METHODOLOGY.md`. Nothing here has been acquired yet — this is the pre-acquisition step. Once a source is sourced into `agent_bibliography/monetary_policy/` (naming convention: `topic_description (Author, Year).pdf`), process it into a new `monetary_policy_conceptual_map.md`, one source at a time, following the same pattern used for `exchange_rate_conceptual_map.md`.

**Scope:** core monetary policy theory + Brazil-specific application + international spillovers relevant to EM monetary policy autonomy (per the agreed scope). Does not duplicate the exchange-rate-specific literature already covered in `exchange_rate_policy/` (e.g. UIP failure, currency regimes) — where a topic touches both (e.g. inflation targeting under exchange rate volatility), it's included here because the primary lens is monetary policy, not FX.

**Foundational score:** 1-5, defined in `BIBLIOGRAPHY_METHODOLOGY.md` §c. Shown next to publication year so canonicity and recency stay distinct — a 5 from 1977 (Kydland & Prescott) is not "outdated," it's the reason the field exists in its current form.

30 literature candidates across 7 clusters, plus a primary-source track (§8) for COPOM's own recurring communications. One addition (Minella et al., 2002, BCB Working Paper No. 53) was moved in from the inflation agent's bibliography during a 2026-07 scoping pass — it's about the IT regime's own early progress report, not the inflation process itself, so it belongs here rather than in `inflation_bibliography_candidates.md`.

## 1. `#policy_rules_and_credibility` — rules vs. discretion, time inconsistency, central bank independence

The theoretical spine of modern monetary policy: why "just do the right thing each period" fails, and what institutional fixes (rules, independence, conservative bankers) do about it.

| Source | Type | Score / Year | Why | Suggested filename |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| Friedman, M. (1968), "The Role of Monetary Policy," *American Economic Review* | Paper | 5 / 1968 | Natural rate hypothesis; the classic statement of what monetary policy can and cannot do — predates and motivates the whole rules-vs-discretion debate | `role_of_monetary_policy (Friedman, 1968).pdf` |

| Kydland, F. & Prescott, E. (1977), "Rules Rather than Discretion: The Inconsistency of Optimal Plans," *Journal of Political Economy* | Paper | 5 / 1977 | The founding time-inconsistency result — discretion produces an inflationary bias even with well-intentioned policymakers | `time_inconsistency_optimal_plans (Kydland & Prescott, 1977).pdf` |

| Barro, R. & Gordon, D. (1983), "A Positive Theory of Monetary Policy in a Natural Rate Model," *Journal of Political Economy* | Paper | 5 / 1983 | Formalizes Kydland-Prescott into the workhorse inflation-bias model still taught today | `positive_theory_monetary_policy (Barro & Gordon, 1983).pdf` |

| Rogoff, K. (1985), "The Optimal Degree of Commitment to an Intermediate Monetary Target," *Quarterly Journal of Economics* | Paper | 5 / 1985 | The "conservative central banker" solution to the inflation bias — direct theoretical ancestor of modern central bank mandate design | `conservative_central_banker (Rogoff, 1985).pdf` |

| Cukierman, A. (1992), *Central Bank Strategy, Credibility, and Independence: Theory and Evidence*, MIT Press — **book, get relevant chapters** | Book | 4 / 1992 | Canonical synthesis of time-inconsistency theory with the empirical central bank independence (CBI) indices still used in cross-country studies today. Recommended chapters: the introductory theoretical chapter formalizing commitment/discretion, plus the chapter introducing the legal-independence index (LVAU/LVAW) — confirm exact chapter numbers against the table of contents on acquisition | `central_bank_independence_theory (Cukierman, 1992).pdf` |

| Taylor, J. (1993), "Discretion versus Policy Rules in Practice," *Carnegie-Rochester Conference Series on Public Policy* | Paper | 5 / 1993 | The Taylor rule — still *the* reference for systematic monetary policy rules, taught and applied unchanged in structure 30+ years later | `taylor_rule (Taylor, 1993).pdf` |

| Walsh, C. (1995), "Optimal Contracts for Central Bankers," *American Economic Review* | Paper | 5 / 1995 | The other half of the institutional-design literature alongside Rogoff (1985): solves the inflation bias via an explicit performance contract rather than delegating to a banker with different preferences — the theoretical basis for arrangements like New Zealand's policy-target agreement | `optimal_contracts_central_bankers (Walsh, 1995).pdf` |

## 8. `#brazil_policy_communication` — COPOM primary-source materials

**This cluster is structurally different from clusters 1-7 above and doesn't use the foundational-score framework.** Clusters 1-7 are settled academic literature — a fixed set of past publications where "foundational vs. superseded" is a meaningful axis. COPOM's own communications are a **recurring institutional feed**: roughly 8 meetings/year, each producing a Statement and Minutes, plus a Quarterly Report — there is no "canonical" COPOM Ata, just an ongoing stream, and what matters most is usually the most *recent* releases rather than any single historical one. Purpose here is to let the agent read BCB's own stated reasoning for each decision, directly testing it against the frameworks in clusters 1-7 (e.g. does a given Ata's language read as Svensson-style forecast targeting? Does it show signs of the fiscal-dominance dynamic Blanchard describes?).

| Document | Portuguese name | Cadence | What it contains | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Statement | Comunicado | Same day as each COPOM meeting (~8/year) | Very short (roughly one page): the Selic decision itself plus a brief summary of the immediate rationale and balance of risks | BCB website, COPOM section |
| Minutes | Ata do Copom | ~6 business days after each meeting | Full deliberation: detailed scenario discussion, balance-of-risks analysis, forward guidance language, vote record | BCB website, COPOM section |
| Quarterly Report | Relatório de Política Monetária (RPM) — named *Relatório de Inflação* before a 2023 rename | Quarterly (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec) | Full projection exercise: conditional and unconditional inflation projections, fan charts (leques), GDP projections, scenario assumptions (FX, oil, Selic path), special-topic boxes | BCB website, COPOM/publications section |
