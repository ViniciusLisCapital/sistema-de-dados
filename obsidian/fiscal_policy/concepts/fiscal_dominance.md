# Fiscal Dominance

**Type:** Macroeconomic regime / theoretical concept
**Tags:** #fiscal-dominance #monetary-policy #inflation #debt #bcb #brasil

---

## Definition

A situation where the deterioration of public finances **limits or undermines the central bank's ability to control inflation** — and may even produce the opposite effect to what is intended.

Fiscal dominance is not binary. It exists on a continuous spectrum:

```
Normal times ←————————————————→ Fiscal Dominance
↓ rate → ↓ inflation          ↓ rate → ↑ inflation (Blanchard mechanism)
```

---

## Theoretical framework

### Sargent-Wallace (1981) — "Unpleasant Monetarist Arithmetic"

When fiscal policy "dominates," the CB is eventually forced to monetize uncontrolled debt growth, fueling inflation even if initially restrictive. Requires: debt trajectory independent of monetary policy; CB unable to discipline the fiscal authority.

### Leeper (1991) — Regime matrix

| | Monetary Active | Monetary Passive |
|---|---|---|
| Fiscal Irresponsible | Hyperinflation + debt explosion | **Fiscal Dominance** |
| Fiscal Responsible | **Inflation targeting** | No anchor |

### Blanchard (2004) — Continuous spectrum

Rate hike → ↑ probability of debt default → ↑ risk premium (ρ) → capital outflows → currency depreciation → ↑ inflation. The monetary transmission is reversed when the fiscal situation is sufficiently deteriorated. Key insight: fiscal dominance exists as a **spectrum**, not a threshold.

### Fiscal Theory of the Price Level (FTPL)

```
D_t / P_t = E_t Σ [(T_{t+i} - G_{t+i}) / (1+r)^i]
```

Deficits not offset by future surpluses equilibrate via inflation eroding the real value of debt.

### Woodford (1996) — Wealth effect

Fiscal expansion → ↑ private net wealth → ↑ domestic demand → inflationary pressure, even with an active CB. Credible debt limits are necessary and sufficient to prevent this channel.

---

## Two conditions — and why one is enough

1. **Passive central bank** — hesitant to fight inflation, accommodating fiscal policy
2. **Irresponsible fiscal policy** — no credible limits on the public debt trajectory

Condition 2 alone can prevent inflation stabilization even when the CB is fully active (Woodford channel). A central bank acting correctly is **necessary but not sufficient**.

Three information sets:

| Set | What it measures | Key variables |

## BRL / application to the current analysis

Brazil (January 2025):
- BCB remains active (Selic at/above Taylor Rule) → condition 1 not yet met
- Fiscal credibility significantly deteriorated (debt/GDP 72% → 85%, no convergence) → condition 2 partially met
- Risk premium elevated; BRL depreciated despite high Selic
- Blanchard mechanism: rate hikes not yet causing depreciation, but offsetting carry inflows that should have driven appreciation

Itaú's assessment: Brazil in an intermediate regime, moving toward fiscal dominance. The elevated BRL risk premium is the market price of condition 2.
